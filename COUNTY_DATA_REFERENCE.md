# County Data Reference

## Mau Forest Complex

### Location
The Mau Forest Complex spans across 4 counties:
1. **Nakuru County**
2. **Kericho County**
3. **Narok County**
4. **Bomet County**

### Area Measurements
- **Total Area**: 416,000 hectares (1,027,954 acres)
- **Significance**: Largest indigenous montane forest in East Africa

### Key Facts
- Critical water catchment area
- Feeds major rivers: Mara, Sondu, Ewaso Ngiro
- Biodiversity hotspot
- Indigenous forest ecosystem

## Summary Statistics

### ASAL Coverage
- **Total ASAL Area**: 51,610,000 hectares (127,520,379 acres)
- **Total Counties Affected**: 23 counties
- **Arid Counties**: 9 (mainly northern and northeastern Kenya)
- **Semi-Arid Counties**: 14 (mainly central, eastern, and coastal Kenya)

### National Impact
- ASAL areas cover approximately **80-89%** of Kenya's total land mass
- Home to **36-38%** of Kenya's population
- Support **70%** of Kenya's national livestock
- Critical for pastoralist and agro-pastoralist communities

### Climate Characteristics

**Arid Areas**:
- Annual rainfall: 200-400mm
- High temperatures
- Extreme water scarcity
- Prone to severe droughts

**Semi-Arid Areas**:
- Annual rainfall: 400-600mm
- Moderate to high temperatures
- Seasonal water availability
- Mixed farming possible in some areas

## Data Sources & Notes

1. **Area measurements**: Approximate values based on Kenya National Bureau of Statistics
2. **ASAL classification**: Based on Kenya's National Drought Management Authority (NDMA) classifications
3. **County boundaries**: From Kenya Open Data Initiative GeoJSON files
4. **Mau Forest data**: Approximate coordinates and area measurements

### Important Notes
- Some counties may have both arid and fertile areas; classifications represent predominant characteristics
- Area measurements may vary slightly depending on the source
- For official planning, consult Kenya National Bureau of Statistics and NDMA
